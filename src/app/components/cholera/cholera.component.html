Cholera Diagnosis
Correct Diagnosis For Your Symptoms
Symptoms Below

Patient Group

  • Mild-moderate volume depletion, without vomiting
  • Lethargy or Laziness or Fatigue
  • Watery diarrhea
  • Vomitting
  • Volume depletion or dehydration
  • Abdominal pain or stomach pain
  • S-typhi and S-paratyphi infections
Treatment Options
Primary Options

Azithromycin: Children: 20 mg/kg orally as a single dose, maximum 1000 mg/dose;
Adults: 1000 mg orally as a single dose

OR

Tetracycline: Children ≥8 years of age: 12.5 mg/kg orally four times daily for 3 days, maximum 500 mg/dose;
Adults: 500 mg orally four times daily for 3 days

OR

Doxycycline: children ≥8 years of age: 6 mg/kg/day orally/intravenously as a single dose;
Adults: 300 mg orally/intravenously as a single dose

OR

Trithethoprim/Sulfamethoxazole: Children: 4-5 mg/kg lorally twice daily for 3 days;
Adults: 160 me orally twice
